Improving Accuracy: Tuning Pronunciations

The recognizer needs to know how to pronounce the words in the recognition vocabulary. The key to good recognition is to accurately model word pronunciations and their variations. In a speaker-independent system, there may be many ways that different speakers say the same words. The extent to which we can capture the pronunciation variability of target users will impact recognition accuracy. However, allowing for too much variability increases the complexity of the recognizer, which results in lower accuracy. Only those pronunciation alternates that are statistically significant should be added.

A common source of pronunciations is a commercial dictionary. Some dictionaries are better than others and some will list idealized or proper pronunciations that are seldom used by speakers of the language. E.g. the word deleted might be listed with a pronunciation such as /d I l i: th ^ d/, when it is usually pronounced /d I l i: th_( & d/. Notice the proper t that is usually flapped and the final vowel which changes from its proper pronouncement as a back vowel to a under-emphasized schwa. Accuracy will improve when your pronunciations are closer to that actually used by speakers.

It is recommended that you specify pronunciations explicitly when defining a vocabulary. This allows you to modify and refine them during the application development process. Pronunciation tuning is best done empirically as either a batch process or using live input. It would typically involve carefully reviewing cases where the recognizer yields the incorrect result, paying attention to both the pronunciation of the target phrase and actual phrase recognized and modifying them as appropriate. In highly confusable cases, this may involve actually modifying vocabulary itself. Note that the recognizer can be configured to return the actual phonemes recognized, which can be useful during the development and tuning process (see thfRecogInit). Through an iterative process, it's usually possible to improve overall accuracy significantly by addressing frequent erroneous cases.

NOTE: Pronunciation tuning requires familiarity with both the Syntax of Pronunciations and with the Worldbet phonetic alphabet (see language-specific documentation for a list of Worldbet symbols supported by a particular language pack).
