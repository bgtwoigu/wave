Audio Support

You should consider using a separate thread for capturing audio and for performing pipelined recognition (see How to Recognize). This allows you to utilize operating system scheduling to ensure adequate cycles for the capturing thread so that you don't miss any audio samples, while utilizing any available cycles to finish recognition as soon as the end of the utterance is detected. Depending on your needs, audio recording and playback can become complicated especially if you require asynchronous control. Refer to the RecogPipe Sample for an example of how to do pipelined recognition using your own audio interface.

In general, the TrulyHandsfree SDK requires mono 16-bit linear PCM audio format for input and output. The required sample rate depends on which acoustic model is used by the recognizer, which you must specify when you create the recognizer (see Create a recognizer). Some conversion to and from this format may be required by your application, such as converting from mu-law to linear PCM, or converting the sample rate.

Audio-related API functions include:

  • thfWaveFromFile: reads a RIFF (sometimes referred to as Windows audio format or .wav) file into memory.
  • thfWaveSaveToFile: saves audio to a RIFF file.
  • thfRecogGetSampleRate: reports the sample rate of recognizer.
  • thfRecogSampleConvert: converts audio sample rate to match the recognizer.
