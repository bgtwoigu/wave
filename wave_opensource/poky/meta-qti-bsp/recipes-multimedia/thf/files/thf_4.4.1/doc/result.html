Getting the Recognition Result

Use thfRecogResult to get the recognition result. It returns the recognized word or phrase and a confidence score. The confidence score reflects how well the input speech matched the underlying grammar and pronunciation model. Typically, a score >=1.0 is regarded as a good result and <1.0 as a poor result. The actual confidence score calculation is dependent on the Garbage parameter.

If your grammar is set up to detect out-of-vocabulary input, you can reject the utterance if the highest scoring result is *nota.

Call thfRecogResult multiple times in order to retrieve the top N results, referred to as N-best. The N-best results may include duplicate phrases that differ with respect to their scores and alignment. When there are no (more) recognition results available, it will return NULL and a score of 0.0.

The Beam and Pruning parameters (see Parameters) affect how many results are available.

The start and end times of the speech identified by the speech detector can be obtained by calling thfRecogGetSpeechRange. Also, use thfRecogGetClipping to determine whether the audio input was clipped (i.e., too loud).
