How to Detect Speech

The recognizer can be configured to use an internal speech detector as a front end to the recognition process (see thfRecogCreateFromFile). As the name suggests, the purpose of a speech detector is to distinguish speech from non-speech events. The intent is for it to act as a form of audio filter such that we only perform speech recognition on the speech regions. Not only does this reduce processing requirements (the recognizer being more computationally expensive than the speech detector), it can also improve recognition accuracy and reduce false alarms.

More specifically, blocks of audio are fed through the speech detector. Each block is identified as either speech or non-speech. Typically, non-speech audio blocks are discarded until speech activity is detected. Subsequently contiguous speech blocks are passed onto the recognizer, until the end of utterance is detected. The end of utterance is determined by observing sufficient silence following a speech region.

Several speech detector parameters that can be queried and configured using thfRecogConfigGet and thfRecogConfigSet.
