Overview of the Recognizer

This section will guide you through the process of using the SDK to build and run a speech recognizer.

Conceptually, a speech recognizer is simply an algorithm that takes digitized audio as input and produces a text representation of the recognized words as output. In practice, there are a number of assumptions that are typically made to achieve this, such as what language we're dealing with and what's the set of possible words or phrases we're expected to recognize. The expected sequence of words is known as the recognition task and consists of a grammar specification and a list of words and their pronunciations.

Sensory's TrulyHandsfree recognition engine is made up of two parts:

  • Acoustic module: This performs acoustic processing of the audio input, including speech detection, signal processing, feature extraction and phoneme probability estimation. It requires an acoustic model in the form of a neural network datafile that must be loaded during initialization. This datafile determines the language-specific and speaker-independent characteristics of the recognizer. The (hidden) data structure representing the acoustic module is referred to as the recog object.
  • Search module: This processes the phoneme probability estimates with respect to the vocabulary specification to determine the highest scoring word sequence. It requires a pre-compiled grammar and vocabulary specification that identifies the allowable word and pronunciations sequences. The compilation can occur off-line or just in time (online). The (hidden) data structure representing the search module is referred to as the search object.
